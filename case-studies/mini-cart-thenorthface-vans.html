Mini Cart Redesign

A mini cart redesign for The North Face and Vans.

The Problem

The existing mini cart had several problems identified by our brand partners. Customers did not have the ability to remove cart items, the close icon wasn't in a typical placement, and it was hypothesized that there was cognitive load from having two CTA buttons. Our goal was to address these issues and revamp the mini cart.

The Solution

North Face

Vans

All designs owned by VF Corporation

The Process

Competitive Analysis

Our research team did a competitive analysis and charted out mini-cart features that were used or not used by 12 simlilar companies. There wasn't a consensus between all the brands and the features they used. This information helped drive the need for usability testing.

Ideation Workshop

We held an ideation workshop to explore various design solutions while collaborating and designing in real time with stakeholders, developers, and the product owner. We emerged with solid ideas and some questions.

Key areas of focus:

  • Mini cart header
  • Free shipping threshold
  • Delete functionality
  • Savings messaging
  • Cart vs Checkout CTA
  • Upsell section

Usability Testing Results

Our researcher conducted the testing and sythensized the results. Concept 1 performed the best.

Key areas of focus results:

  • Mini cart header

    Users understood the header copy and closing the panel was not an issue.

  • Free shipping threshold

    The savings threshold made sense to the users. There was confusing during the testing.

  • Savings messaging

    The savings messaging worked well. Users liked that it was hightlighted as that was the most important aspect of the mini cart for them. That along with confirmation that the product was added to the cart.

  • Delete functionality

    The delete icon tested well. Users were familiar with the icon which eliminated the option to use remove copy.

  • Cart vs Checkout CTA

    Most users perferred to go to the cart. Based on discussions with stakholders we decided to move forward with one View Cart CTA.

  • Upsell section

    The users stated that they would only be interested in the upsell items if the price was low and therfore could get them over the free shipping threshold.
