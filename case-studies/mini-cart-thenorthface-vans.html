Mini Cart Redesign

A redesign of the mini cart for The North Face and Vans

Find local FoodTrucks, Order on app, Pick up food, Done.

The Problem

The existing mini cart had several problems identified by our brand partners. Customers did not have the ability to remove cart items, the close icon wasn't in a typical placement, and it was hypothesized that there was cognitive load from having two CTA buttons. Our goal was to address these issues and revamp the mini cart.

The Solution

Vans

The Process

Competitive Analysis

Our research team did a competitive analysis and charted out what mini-cart features were used or not used by 12 of our competitors.

Ideation Workshop

We conducted an ideation workshop exploring various design solutions with stakeholders, developers, and the product owner. We left with solid ideas and some questions.

Key areas of focus:

  • Header
  • Savings threshold
  • Delete functionality
  • Savings messaging
  • Cart vs Checkout CTA
  • Upsell section

UI Elements

For consistency and familiarity, I used a number of stand UI elements and the iOS SF font.

Color Psychology

Red is known to invoke hunger so I chose it as my primary color. I used a light shade of yellow as the secondary color since it is warm and also can stimulate the appetite.

Conclusion

The project was a collaborative success. We did research to back up every design decision. The updated mini cart is live on The North Face and Vans. The brands will monitor the performance and iterate if necessary.
